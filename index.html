Front Foot Pickup Before Forward Movement

Picks up front foot before moving forward rather than pushing through the ground. This demonstrates quad weakness - the brain defaults to using reactivity and larger range of motion instead of direct powerful push. Results in delayed acceleration and reduced force production in critical first steps.

Knee Collapse During Acceleration

Knee collapsing inward during contact phase indicates insufficient quad and glute strength to maintain proper knee alignment. This internal rotation at the hip reduces force transfer efficiency and increases injury risk. Needs targeted strength work to stabilize the knee joint during ground contact.

Landing in Front of COM with Heel Strike

Foot landing significantly in front of center of mass with prominent heel contact creates braking forces. This overstriding pattern, combined with heel striking, slows acceleration and increases ground contact time. Needs to focus on forefoot landing under the hips for more efficient force production.

Rotation & Alignment Issues

Knees pointing out too much with large rotation in legs during recovery phase. Taking him side-to-side rather than straight forward, reducing speed efficiency. Needs hips, toes, and legs more aligned straight in acceleration direction to maximize forward propulsion.

Heel Strike & Hamstring Load (Injury Risk)

Reaching for ground instead of placing foot down, causing flatfooted heel striking with foot landing in front of center of mass (similar to Khloe). This places significant load on hamstring and creates strain over time - injury risk. Must transition to foot placement under center of mass with forefoot contact.

Development Pathway: Power & Coordination

Primary focus: Add strength to stabilize knees on impact and develop overall power output. As she gets stronger, can activate more muscle fibers for greater push and aggression in acceleration. Need more stiffness and tension to complement her excellent mobility. Get more coordinated at speed work to bring foot/leg down quicker rather than reaching in front.

Heel Striking in Front of Center of Mass

Significant heel striking with foot landing far in front of center of mass creates massive braking forces with each step. This landing pattern forces deceleration rather than acceleration, dramatically limiting top speed potential and efficiency.

Knee Deforming at Impact

Severe knee deformation on contact showing significant quad weakness. Knee breaking/collapsing indicates insufficient strength to maintain stiffness through contact phase. This reduces force transfer efficiency and contributes to slower ground contact time (150ms vs Khloe's 130ms).

Limited Arm Extension

Restricted arm extension limiting drive and forward propulsion. Arms not achieving full range of motion needed to generate momentum and counterbalance leg action. This mechanical restriction reduces overall sprint efficiency and power output.

Braking Force Creating Backside Mechanics

Heel striking too far in front of COM creates severe braking forces and backside mechanics on the back leg, causing active deceleration with each step rather than continuous acceleration. This compound issue - landing far in front combined with pushing off behind the body - creates a push-brake cycle that severely limits velocity development.

Developing Ground Contact Time

150ms GCT at top speed - significantly slower than Khloe (130ms) and far from elite targets (~90ms). Needs reactive strength development to reduce ground contact time while increasing force production.

Too Much Vertical Rise & Air Time

Going too vertical with excessive air time instead of staying forward. Needs to maintain more forward lean/projection to keep velocity horizontal rather than losing energy to vertical displacement. More forward body position will reduce air time and increase ground contact frequency.

Development Priority: Speed Coordination & Stiffness

Focus on speed-specific coordination drills to improve foot placement and timing. Add stiffness throughout kinetic chain while maintaining her excellent mobility. Key improvements: forefoot striking under center of mass, reducing side-to-side motion, creating more forward projection. Her power base is strong - needs technical refinement.
